<br> #### Class 5: 3D Design, Printing, and Scanning For my 3D-printing assignment, I decided to create a hinge shape that I would need for my final project. I was very hesitant to create this shape because I did not know how well the 3D-printer would be able to handle it, but I went ahead and attempted it anyways. First, I sketched out the basic design in Fusion 360. (circle = 10mm, total length = 20mm) <img src=../pictures/hpic1.png width="380" height="250"> <img src=../pictures/hpic2.png width="380" height="250"> <p>Next, I added a 5mm circle on one sketch and a 4.5mm circle on the other. </p> <p> <img src=../pictures/hpic3.png width="380" height="250"> <p>I had the basic sketch complete so then I extruded the center part of the hinge by 5mm (the side with the 5mm hole in the center). Then, I extruded the other sketch by 2mm and the 4.5mm center by 6mm. After that, I mirrored the sketch on the other side to create the fork-like shape below. </p> <img src=../pictures/hpic4.png width="380" height="250"> <img src=../pictures/hpic5.png width="380" height="250"> <p>Once I had both shapes complete, I moved them together and submitted the hinge to the 3D-printer. </p> <p> <img src=../pictures/hpic6.png width="380" height="250"> <p>My hinge took about 15 minutes to print, and it turned out surprisingly well! The hinge had an entire 360 degrees of rotation without separation, getting stuck, or breaking.
